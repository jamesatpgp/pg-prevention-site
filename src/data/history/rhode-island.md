# Rhode Island — Gambling History

## The present

Rhode Island in 2026 has a small, vertically integrated gambling market wholly tied to the **Rhode Island Lottery** and its single private operator partner. Legal forms include: two commercial casinos (Bally's Twin River Lincoln and Bally's Tiverton), both operated by Bally's Corporation under Lottery license and offering video lottery terminals, table games, and retail sportsbooks; statewide mobile sports betting (since September 2019, operated by IGT under the Lottery's exclusive vendor contract, marketed as "Sportsbook Rhode Island"); statewide iGaming (since March 5, 2024, with Bally's as the Lottery's exclusive vendor); the state Lottery's draw, instant, and Keno products; simulcast pari-mutuel wagering at the casinos; and charitable bingo and raffles. There is **no live racing** (the last greyhound meet ran August 8, 2009; Narragansett Park closed in 1978). Rhode Island is the only U.S. state where every form of regulated commercial gambling sits under the Lottery's "lotteries operated by the state" constitutional exception (R.I. Const. art. VI, § 15), administered with one master vendor per channel.¹ ²

## How it got here

Rhode Island's colonial Charter (1663) was unusually tolerant, and the colonial assembly authorized lotteries for Brown University (1764), Trinity Church, and bridges. The 1843 Constitution prohibited lotteries except where the General Assembly approved by two-thirds vote of each house **and** voters ratified at statewide referendum — a uniquely Rhode Island procedural gate that has shaped every subsequent expansion.³

The General Assembly authorized **pari-mutuel wagering on dog racing in 1934** (Public Laws of 1934, ch. 2086). Narragansett Park (Thoroughbred, Pawtucket) opened that year and ran until 1978; Lincoln Greyhound Park opened in 1947; Newport Jai Alai opened in 1976 under separate pari-mutuel jai alai authorization. Live greyhound racing ended in 2009 after voters approved Question 4 of November 2008 and the General Assembly confirmed in 2009 legislation. Charitable bingo was authorized in 1937 — among the earliest in the country.⁴

The modern Lottery arrived via a **November 1973 constitutional amendment**; first ticket sold May 30, 1974, making Rhode Island the fourth-oldest modern state lottery. **VLTs at the pari-mutuel facilities** (Newport Jai Alai and Lincoln Greyhound Park) were authorized in **1992** — structured as Lottery-operated terminals to fit within the "lotteries operated by the state" exception. Twin River Lincoln (renamed from Lincoln Greyhound Park) and Newport Grand expanded through the 2000s. **Table games** were voter-approved at Twin River in **November 2012** and launched June 2013; Tiverton's Bally's Tiverton Casino Hotel opened **September 1, 2018**, replacing Newport Grand (which closed in August 2018) after voter approval in November 2016. Twin River Worldwide Holdings acquired the Bally's brand in 2020 and rebranded the parent and both properties.

After *Murphy v. NCAA*, 138 S. Ct. 1461 (2018), the General Assembly enacted sports-wagering legislation in June 2018 (Public Laws of 2018, ch. 47), authorizing the Rhode Island Lottery — through the casinos and its existing IGT vendor relationship — to offer retail (launched November 26, 2018) and **mobile** sports betting (launched September 4, 2019). The 2023 iGaming law (P.L. 2023, ch. 309, signed June 22, 2023) authorized Bally's as the Lottery's exclusive iGaming vendor; iGaming launched **March 5, 2024**, making Rhode Island the seventh U.S. state to authorize online casino gaming and the second New England state to launch (after Connecticut).⁵ Rhode Island captures a high share of gross sports-wagering revenue (51% state share) but maintains very limited operator competition — a deliberate single-vendor design rather than a market failure.

## Regulatory framework today

- **Rhode Island Lottery (Department of Revenue, Division of Lotteries)** — Lottery games, VLTs, casino table games, sports wagering (retail and mobile), iGaming, **and the statewide voluntary self-exclusion list**. https://www.rilot.com/
- **Rhode Island Department of Business Regulation, Division of Gaming and Athletics Licensing & Regulation** — operator and key-employee licensing.
- **Department of Behavioral Healthcare, Developmental Disabilities and Hospitals (BHDDH)** — gambling-disorder treatment funding. https://bhddh.ri.gov/
- **Bally's Corporation** — operator of both casinos (Twin River Lincoln and Tiverton); IGT — Lottery's central system and sports-wagering technology partner.

## Voluntary self-exclusion

Rhode Island runs a **single statewide voluntary self-exclusion list administered by the Lottery** that covers every regulated channel — casinos, VLTs, retail and mobile sports betting, and iGaming. The single-operator, single-vendor structure of Rhode Island gaming means a single enrollment is functionally complete.⁶

**Administering agency.** Rhode Island Lottery (Division of Lotteries, Department of Revenue), with operational support from Bally's at the casinos.

**Where to enroll.** Two paths:

1. **Online** through the Lottery's voluntary self-exclusion portal, with identity verification.
2. **In person** by submitting a written application to the **Bally's Lincoln Casino Resort Security department** at 100 Twin River Road, Lincoln, RI 02865, or at Bally's Tiverton Casino Hotel Security.

**Duration.** **1 year, 5 years, or lifetime** (permanent).

**Scope.** A single enrollment covers all three of: (a) the two Bally's casinos (VLTs, table games, retail sportsbooks); (b) the Lottery's mobile sports-betting product (Sportsbook RI) and any subsequent licensed mobile-betting product; (c) the Lottery's iGaming product operated by Bally's. The Lottery's retail draw and instant games — Powerball, Mega Millions, scratch tickets, Keno at retailers — are **not** point-of-sale enforced because retailers do not check ID against the list. iLottery account creation is blocked.

**Removal.** Removal is by affirmative written request at the end of a 1- or 5-year term; lifetime is permanent.

**Penalties for breach.** Self-excluded persons are barred from collecting winnings or comps; winnings are surrendered; the operator may eject and trespass the participant; breach can result in criminal trespass.

**Multi-state.** Rhode Island does not participate in a formal multi-state compact. Bally's and IGT apply cross-state corporate exclusion within their own networks as a matter of policy.

**Help.** **Rhode Island Problem Gambling Helpline 1-877-9-GAMBLER** (1-877-942-6253), 24/7. Sources: Bally's Lincoln Responsible Gaming page (https://casinos.ballys.com/lincoln/responsible-gaming.htm); Rhode Island Lottery responsible gaming pages (https://www.rilot.com/); AGA Rhode Island Fact Sheet 2025 (https://www.americangaming.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/Rhode-Island_AGA-Gaming-Regulatory-Fact-Sheet-2025.pdf).

## Problem gambling response

Rhode Island's problem-gambling funding has historically lagged per-capita gambling revenue, but a series of statutory dedications via the 1992 VLT law, the 2018 sports-betting act, and the 2023 iGaming act have grown the dedicated stream. **BHDDH** administers state gambling-treatment funding through contracted providers; the regional NCPG-affiliated **Council on Problem Gambling of New England** has long served Rhode Island, supplemented at times by a state-level Rhode Island Council on Problem Gambling. NCPG State of the States surveys have repeatedly flagged Rhode Island's funding-to-revenue gap.⁷
