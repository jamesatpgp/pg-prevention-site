Vermont was admitted as the 14th state in 1791, and its early Congregationalist political culture was broadly hostile to commercial gambling. Early statutes criminalized lotteries and common gambling houses. Pari-mutuel wagering on horse racing was legalized in 1961. Green Mountain Race Track in Pownal opened in 1963 — Vermont's only commercial pari-mutuel facility — and in 1976 became the first U.S. track to host thoroughbred, standardbred, and greyhound racing in a single calendar year. Thoroughbreds ended that year, standardbreds in 1977, and Green Mountain ran as a dedicated greyhound facility until live racing ended in 1992. Vermont enacted a statutory prohibition on greyhound racing in 1995. The Green Mountain grandstand burned down in September 2020.

Vermont voters approved a statewide lottery in 1976. The Vermont Lottery Commission was created in 1977, and the first Green Mountain Game ticket sold February 14, 1978. Vermont joined Maine and New Hampshire in 1985 to launch the Tri-State Megabucks, the first multi-state lottery game in U.S. history. The 2018 merger of the Lottery Commission into the Department of Liquor and Lottery consolidated state regulation. Sports betting was legalized by Act 63 of 2023, signed by Governor Phil Scott on June 14, 2023. Mobile sports wagering with DraftKings, FanDuel, and Fanatics went live at midnight on January 11, 2024 — timed to the NFL playoffs. Vermont has no commercial casinos and no tribal gaming.
