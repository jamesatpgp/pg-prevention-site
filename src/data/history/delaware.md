Delaware was one of only two states without a constitutional lottery ban by 1890. The 1897 Delaware Constitution allowed lotteries "under State control," and pari-mutuel horse racing was authorized by the 1933 Horse Racing Act. Delaware Park opened in 1937. The Delaware Lottery was established by the General Assembly in 1974, and its first game in March 1975 was a weekly lottery tied to live horse races at Brandywine and Harrington. In 1976, the lottery offered NFL "Scoreboard" parlay cards. Wagering ended after one season, but the statutory authorization stayed on the books — a small detail that would matter sixteen years later.

That 1976 footprint mattered because of PASPA, the 1992 federal law banning new state sports betting. Delaware's existing sports-lottery statute was grandfathered in. Casino-style gaming began with the 1994 Video Lottery Terminal Act, which authorized slots at the three racetracks; slots launched in late 1995. Table games followed in 2010. The Delaware Gaming Competitiveness Act of 2012 authorized online casino gaming, which went live on October 31, 2013 — one of the first three U.S. states to launch regulated iGaming. After the Supreme Court struck down PASPA in May 2018, Delaware moved fast: full single-game sports betting launched June 5, 2018, the first state after Nevada. Online sports betting followed in December 2023.
