West Virginia separated from Virginia and was admitted as the 35th state on June 20, 1863. Pari-mutuel wagering on horse racing was legalized in 1933 — the Depression-era window that also produced legalization in Texas and Washington. Wheeling Downs opened in 1937, followed by Waterford Park (later Mountaineer) and Charles Town Races. A 1975 dog-racing bill allowed Wheeling Downs to convert to greyhound racing in 1976. Tri-State Greyhound Park (now Mardi Gras Casino) opened in Cross Lanes in 1985. West Virginia remained the last U.S. state with operating commercial greyhound racing after Florida ended its industry on December 31, 2020, and Arkansas's Southland closed on December 31, 2022. Multiple legislative efforts to phase out the industry have failed.

The state lottery was authorized by constitutional amendment in November 1984; the first scratch tickets sold January 9, 1986. The Racetrack Video Lottery Act of 1994 authorized video lottery terminals at the racetracks. The Limited Video Lottery Act of 2001 extended the model to bars and fraternal organizations. Voters approved live table games at the racinos in 2007-2008; tables launched July 1, 2008. The Greenbrier Casino Club opened in 2010 as a private resort casino. Senate Bill 415 of March 2018 anticipated the Murphy decision; retail sportsbooks opened in August 2018 and online sports betting in December 2018. The West Virginia Lottery Interactive Wagering Act of 2019 authorized online casino gaming, and iGaming launched in July 2020.
