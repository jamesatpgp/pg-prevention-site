The Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI: Saipan, Tinian, Rota, and the Northern Islands) passed from Spanish to German (1899), Japanese (1919-1944), and U.S. (1947-1986) control under the U.N. Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands. The Covenant establishing a Commonwealth in political union with the United States took full effect in 1986. Commercial gaming policy is a post-Covenant creation, organized around an island-by-island, senatorial-district structure under NMI Constitution Article XXI. The Tinian Casino Gaming Control Act of 1989 authorized casino gaming on Tinian and created the Tinian Casino Gaming Control Commission. The Tinian Dynasty Hotel & Casino was the principal property for most of the 1990s and 2010s before closing.

Public Law 18-56 of 2014 authorized an exclusive casino license in the Third Senatorial District (Saipan and the Northern Islands) and created the Commonwealth Casino Commission. In August 2014 the license was granted to Imperial Pacific International (CNMI) LLC for a 25-year term. Imperial Pacific opened a temporary casino in Garapan in July 2015, eventually moving to the Imperial Pacific Resort. The Saipan experience was deeply troubled: FBI investigations, labor and construction-finance scandals, and unmet license-fee obligations followed. Operations were suspended for COVID-19 on March 17, 2020. In May 2021, the Commission suspended IPI's casino license after IPI failed to pay required fees and meet capital requirements. Executive Order 2025-002 abolished the Commonwealth Casino Commission and transferred its authority to the Commonwealth Lottery Commission. Cockfighting was preempted by the 2018 Farm Bill effective December 2019.
