Illinois's gambling culture traces to French colonial card and dice games on the Mississippi and Illinois rivers. After statehood in 1818, Chicago grew into a regional center of faro banks, policy and numbers operations, and commercial gambling houses tightly woven into municipal politics. During Prohibition (1920-1933), Al Capone's Outfit became the dominant operator across Cook County and downstate. The 1927 Illinois Horse Racing Act legalized pari-mutuel wagering on horse racing — a compromise that taxed the most popular form while continuing to prohibit others. Through the mid-20th century, illegal handbooks, policy wheels, and slot machines persisted in "wide open" downstate towns and in Cicero and Berwyn; the 1950 Kefauver Senate hearings documented the scale.

Modern expansion came in waves. The Illinois Lottery launched July 1, 1974 and became the first U.S. lottery to sell tickets online in 2012. The Riverboat Gambling Act, signed February 7, 1990, made Illinois the second state after Iowa to authorize riverboat casinos. The Argosy Casino Alton opened first, on September 11, 1991. The cruising requirement was repealed in 1999. The Video Gaming Act of 2009 authorized video gaming terminals in bars, restaurants, truck stops, and fraternal clubs; the first machines went live September 10, 2012. By the mid-2020s Illinois had roughly 8,000 VGT establishments. Public Act 101-0031 of June 2019 authorized six new casino licenses including a Chicago casino, converted racetracks into racinos, and legalized sports wagering. The first legal Illinois sports wager was placed March 9, 2020.
