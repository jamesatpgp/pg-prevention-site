Iowa pioneered two of the modern American casino models. The 1857 Iowa Constitution banned lotteries, and the state held that line through Prohibition and the postwar decades. Pari-mutuel wagering on horse and dog racing was authorized in 1983, creating the Iowa Racing Commission. The Iowa Lottery was authorized by 1984 constitutional amendment and began ticket sales August 22, 1985. Greyhound racing opened at Bluffs Run (1986) and Dubuque (1985). 2014 legislation paid the tracks to retain casino operations in exchange for ending live dog racing; the last meets ran in 2014 and 2015. Three IGRA-compacted tribes operate casinos: the Sac and Fox of the Mississippi in Iowa (Meskwaki, opened December 31, 1992), the Omaha Tribe (Blackbird Bend), and the Winnebago Tribe (WinnaVegas).

Iowa was the first state to legalize riverboat casinos. The Excursion Gambling Boat Act took effect April 1, 1991. Early licensees included the Dubuque Casino Belle, the President Riverboat Casino in Davenport, and the Mississippi Belle II in Clinton. Initial law capped wagers at $5 maximum, losses at $200 per cruise, and required cruising; the caps were loosened in 1994, and the cruising requirement ended in 2004. The 1994 constitutional referendum authorized slots at the racetracks. On April 1, 1995, Prairie Meadows became the first racino in the United States. House File 617 of May 2019 authorized sports wagering; retail and mobile launched August 15, 2019.
