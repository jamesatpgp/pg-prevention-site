Maryland's gambling history runs deeper than most states'. The Maryland Jockey Club, organized in 1743, is one of the oldest sporting organizations in North America. The Preakness Stakes was first run at Pimlico Race Course in 1873 — the second-oldest Triple Crown race. Colonial lotteries financed Maryland roads, bridges, and the Baltimore Washington Monument before the mid-19th-century reform wave shut them down. In 1949, the General Assembly took an unusual step: it authorized slot machines in St. Mary's, Charles, Calvert, and Anne Arundel counties. For nearly two decades, Southern Maryland was known as the "slot machine capital of the East Coast." Corruption scandals tied to local political machines led to a 1968 phase-out.

Modern gambling came back through three constitutional amendments at the ballot box. Voters approved the state lottery by 1972 amendment; tickets went on sale on January 2, 1973. November 2008 Question 2 authorized video lottery terminals at up to five sites; the first five casinos opened between September 2010 and August 2014. November 2012 Question 7 authorized live table games and a sixth casino in Prince George's County. MGM National Harbor opened on December 8, 2016. November 2020 Question 2 authorized sports wagering by constitutional amendment, with implementing legislation in 2021. Retail sports betting launched December 9, 2021, and mobile sports betting launched November 23, 2022.
