Oregon's 1859 constitution included a flat lottery prohibition at Article XV § 4. That ban survived until the 1933 amendment authorizing pari-mutuel wagering on horse races and the 1976 amendment authorizing charitable bingo and raffles. Multnomah Greyhound Park opened in 1944 and ran until 2004, when live greyhound racing ended in the state. The November 1984 Measure 4 authorized the State Lottery; the first scratch tickets sold April 25, 1985. Two distinctive Oregon Lottery products followed. Video lottery terminals at licensed bars and restaurants were authorized in 1991 and remain the Lottery's largest revenue source. Sports Action, an NFL parlay-card game, ran from 1989 to 2007.

Sports Action was the only legal sports wagering in the United States outside Nevada during the PASPA era. Oregon discontinued it in 2007 under NCAA pressure tied to NCAA tournament hosting. Nine federally recognized tribes opened Class III casinos under IGRA compacts beginning in 1992: Cow Creek's Seven Feathers, Chinook Winds (Siletz), Spirit Mountain (Grand Ronde), Wildhorse (Umatilla), and others through 2012. After Murphy v. NCAA, the Oregon Lottery launched Scoreboard — its own mobile-only sports-betting platform — on October 16, 2019, without new legislation, citing its 1985 statutory mandate. In January 2022 the Lottery transitioned to DraftKings as its sole operator. Tribal sportsbooks operate at several casinos under compact amendment. Commercial casinos remain constitutionally prohibited.
