# Northern Mariana Islands — Gambling History

## Current landscape

The Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI: Saipan, Tinian, Rota, and the Northern Islands) today has **statutory casino authorization on Tinian and Saipan but no major operating casino**. The Saipan license held by **Imperial Pacific International (IPI)** has been suspended since 2021 and is in protracted federal bankruptcy proceedings; the Tinian Dynasty Hotel & Casino closed in the 2010s. The **CNMI Lottery** operates under the **Commonwealth Lottery Commission**. In a structural overhaul, **Executive Order 2025-002** abolished the Commonwealth Casino Commission (CCC) and transferred its casino-regulatory authority to the **Commonwealth Lottery Commission**, making the Lottery Commission the single principal gaming regulator in the commonwealth.¹

## Compressed history

The CNMI passed from Spanish to German control in 1899, to Japanese League of Nations mandate (1919–1944), and to U.S. administration under the U.N. Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands (1947–1986). The **Covenant** establishing a Commonwealth in political union with the United States took full effect in 1986. Commercial gaming policy is a post-Covenant creation, organized around an island-by-island, senatorial-district structure under **NMI Constitution Article XXI**, which authorizes the Legislature and local senatorial districts to legalize gaming.²

The **Tinian Casino Gaming Control Act of 1989** (Public Law 6-19) authorized casino gaming on Tinian and created the **Tinian Casino Gaming Control Commission (TCGCC)**; the Tinian Dynasty was the principal property for most of the 1990s–2010s before closing. A Rota Gaming Initiative authorized but never produced a sustained operating casino. **Public Law 18-56 (2014)** — later amended by P.L. 19-24 and P.L. 21-38 — authorized an **exclusive casino license in the Third Senatorial District** (Saipan and the Northern Islands) and created the **Commonwealth Casino Commission**. In August 2014 the license was granted to **Imperial Pacific International (CNMI) LLC** for an initial 25-year term. The IPI subsidiary "Best Sunshine" soft-opened a temporary casino in Garapan in July 2015, eventually moving operations to the **Imperial Pacific Resort**.³

The Saipan casino experience was deeply troubled: FBI investigations publicly reported in March 2017, labor and construction-finance scandals, and unmet license-fee obligations. Operations were suspended on **March 17, 2020** for COVID-19. In **May 2021**, the CCC **suspended IPI's casino license** after IPI failed to pay the $15.5M annual license fee, $3.1M regulatory fee, $20M-per-year community benefit fund contributions, and the $2 billion minimum capital requirement. The CNMI Superior Court upheld the suspension. The CCC effectively shut its office in January 2023 and commissioners had not been paid since 2021. **Executive Order 2025-002** then abolished the CCC, transferring authority — including enforcement of the existing IPI license and negotiation of cure amounts — to the **Commonwealth Lottery Commission**.⁴ Cockfighting was preempted by the **2018 Farm Bill** effective December 2019.

## Regulatory framework today

The **Commonwealth Lottery Commission** is now the single principal gaming regulator, operating under **NMIAC Title 70-50.1** (Lottery Rules) and absorbing residual casino-supervision authority under EO 2025-002. Casino regulations are at **NMIAC Title 175-10.1** (Commonwealth Casino Commission Rules, still operative as standards even though the CCC itself is abolished) and **NMIAC Title 170-30** (TCGCC Rules for Tinian). The **Tinian Casino Gaming Control Commission** — https://opd.gov.mp/library/agency/tinian-casino-gaming-control-commission.html — retains its local statutory authorization. Behavioral-health services are delivered through the **CNMI Commonwealth Healthcare Corporation (CHCC)** — https://chcc.health.

## Voluntary self-exclusion (VSE)

**Administering agency:** Under **Executive Order 2025-002**, voluntary self-exclusion administration has moved from the abolished Commonwealth Casino Commission to the **Commonwealth Lottery Commission**.⁵ The substantive self-exclusion rule remains the one promulgated by the CCC at **NMIAC Title 175-10.1** (Commonwealth Casino Commission Rules and Regulations), which imposes responsible-gaming requirements — including a player self-exclusion procedure — on any licensed casino operator on Saipan. The **Tinian Casino Gaming Control Commission** retains parallel authority at **NMIAC Title 170-30** for Tinian.

**Where to enroll:** With no major casino currently operating on Saipan (IPI suspended) and no operating major casino on Tinian (Tinian Dynasty closed), the practical operating footprint of the program is minimal. The enrollment pathway as written in NMIAC Title 175-10.1 is through the casino licensee's responsible-gaming program with notice to the regulator; following EO 2025-002, the Commonwealth Lottery Commission inherits the registry function. There is no published public-facing online enrollment portal as of 2026.

**Duration options:** Terms are set by the casino licensee's responsible-gaming plan filed under NMIAC Title 175-10.1; CCC-era plans typically followed industry-standard 1-year, 5-year, and lifetime tiers, but the absence of an active major licensee leaves practical enforcement effectively dormant.

**Scope:** Covers licensed casino premises under the CNMI casino licensing regime — Saipan (IPI, suspended) and Tinian licensees under TCGCC. The CNMI Lottery (the only currently active product) operates under separate Lottery Commission rules at NMIAC Title 70-50.1; lottery-specific exclusion is not the same registry.

**Removal process:** Set by licensee plan and Commission rules; not centrally published.

**Penalties:** Licensees that fail to enforce self-exclusion face Commission discipline under NMIAC Title 175-10.1.

**2026 program state:** **Effectively dormant.** With the Saipan casino license suspended since 2021, the principal Tinian casino closed, IPI in federal bankruptcy, and the CCC abolished and replaced by the Commonwealth Lottery Commission per EO 2025-002, the CNMI's voluntary self-exclusion infrastructure exists on paper but lacks an active regulated venue to enforce against. Legislative proposals to authorize **multiple casino licenses** (in lieu of the exclusive license) and to add **iGaming** under a modernized framework continue to receive testimony in the House and Senate Committees on Gaming and would, if enacted, restart the practical relevance of the self-exclusion regime.
