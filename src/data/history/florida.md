In 1931, with the Depression economy collapsing, the Florida Legislature overrode Governor Doyle Carlton's veto to pass the Pari-Mutuel Wagering Act. Florida became the first U.S. state to legalize betting on both horse and dog races. The dog-track sector exploded after 1931: by 1935 Florida had 10 greyhound tracks, and at its peak the state hosted 11 of the 17 operational U.S. greyhound tracks. Jai-alai was legalized in 1935 and operated at frontons in Miami, Tampa, and Dania. Florida even briefly authorized pari-mutuel wagering on midget-car racing in the late 1930s. Voters ended all Florida greyhound racing through Amendment 13 in 2018; live racing stopped December 31, 2020. Jai-alai has wound down to near zero in the mid-2020s.

The lottery prohibition fell with Amendment 5 in November 1986; the Florida Lottery began ticket sales January 12, 1988. The Seminole Tribe opened the first tribal high-stakes bingo hall in the United States at Hollywood in 1979 — the legal predicate for the 1987 Cabazon decision and Congress's 1988 Indian Gaming Regulatory Act. Voters authorized slot machines at pari-mutuel facilities in Broward and Miami-Dade by Amendment 4 in 2004; slots launched between 2006 and 2009. Governor Ron DeSantis signed a 30-year Seminole compact in May 2021 authorizing mobile sports betting under a hub-and-spoke model. After extended federal litigation, Hard Rock Bet relaunched as the state's sole legal mobile sportsbook on December 7, 2023.
