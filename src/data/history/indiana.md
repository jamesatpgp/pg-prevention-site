Indiana entered the Union in 1816 with a constitutional ban on lotteries, preserved verbatim in Article 15 §8 of the 1851 constitution. The clause constrained Indiana gambling policy until voters amended it in 1988. Through the 19th century, gambling existed in saloons, fairgrounds, and back-room games. In the 20th, illegal slot machines, policy houses, and bookmakers operated in factory towns — Gary, Hammond, East Chicago — tied to mid-century organized-crime networks documented in the Kefauver-era investigations. The 1988 constitutional amendment opened the door. The Hoosier Lottery began scratch sales in October 1989 and joined Powerball in 1990. Pari-mutuel betting was legalized in 1989; Hoosier Park opened in Anderson in 1994, and Indiana Grand opened in Shelbyville in 2002.

The Indiana Riverboat Gambling Act of July 1993 authorized ten casino licenses — five on Lake Michigan, five on the Ohio River. Casino Aztar in Evansville opened first, in December 1995. A statutory exception authorized a single inland casino at French Lick, which opened in 2006. 2007 legislation authorized slots at Hoosier Park and Indiana Grand, creating two racinos; 2015 legislation added live table games. The Pokagon Band of Potawatomi opened Four Winds South Bend on January 16, 2018; the tribe's Class III compact was ratified in 2021. House Enrolled Act 1015 of May 2019 authorized commercial sports wagering. Retail launched September 1, 2019, and mobile October 3, 2019. Hard Rock Casino Northern Indiana opened in Gary in 2021.
