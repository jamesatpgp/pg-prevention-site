Through most of the District's history, gambling was prohibited under federal criminal statutes. No chartered colonial lottery operated in the federal city. The District's federal-enclave status — under the plenary authority of Congress, with no commercial-casino zoning and dense federal property concentrated around the National Mall — has shaped every gambling-policy choice. The modern era began with Initiative 6, the "Law to Legalize Lotteries, Daily Numbers Games, and Bingo and Raffles for Charitable Purposes," which District voters approved on November 4, 1980. The DC Lottery launched August 25, 1982, making the District one of the earliest jurisdictions in the post-1964 lottery era.

The District has no commercial casinos, no pari-mutuel racing, and no card rooms. The three legal forms of gambling are the DC Lottery (including iLottery), charitable bingo, and sports wagering. After Murphy v. NCAA, the DC Council passed the Sports Wagering Lottery Amendment Act of 2018. The statute designated the Office of Lottery and Gaming as both regulator and operator of a District-wide mobile platform branded GambetDC, which launched in 2020 and drew sustained criticism. The Sports Wagering Amendment Act of 2024 ended the mobile monopoly, expanding Class A authorization to mobile and online wagering and creating a Class C license for sports-team applicants. All regulation is consolidated in the Office of Lottery and Gaming under the Office of the Chief Financial Officer.
