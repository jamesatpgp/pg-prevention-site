The colonial lottery in Puerto Rico was established in 1814 at the initiative of Don Alejandro Ramírez, the first Intendant. The first drawing was held on September 1, 1814, in the main plaza of San Juan — making it one of the oldest continuously documented lottery operations in the Americas. The lottery struggled and lapsed before being re-established through later municipal lotteries in San Juan, Ponce, and Mayagüez. Cockfighting (galleras) was widespread throughout the Spanish colonial period and carried over after the 1898 Treaty of Paris brought the island under U.S. sovereignty. Modern lottery operations were re-established by Law 465 of May 15, 1947, which created the Lottery Bureau within the Treasury Department (Hacienda).

The modern framework rests on three statutes. Act No. 11 of August 22, 1933 (the "Games of Chance Act") governs the traditional lottery. Act No. 221 of May 15, 1948 legalized hotel casinos as a tourism-development measure, requiring operation inside qualified hotels. Act No. 81 of 2019 consolidated regulation in a new seven-member Gaming Commission, which absorbed functions previously housed in the Tourism Company. The Gaming Commission promulgated Sports Betting Regulation 9316 and Fantasy Contest Regulation 9317; mobile sports wagering launched in 2023 with Caesars among the first operators live on island. Cockfighting was effectively prohibited when the 2018 Farm Bill extended the federal Animal Welfare Act to U.S. territories. Thoroughbred horse racing continues at Camarero Racetrack under the Horse Racing Industry and Sport Administration.
