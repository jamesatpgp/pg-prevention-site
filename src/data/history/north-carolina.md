Colonial North Carolina was generally hostile to commercial gambling. The General Assembly's 1834 lottery ban was followed by an even harder line in the 1868 Reconstruction Constitution and the 1876 revision: a flat constitutional ban on lotteries that survived without amendment for 129 years. Charitable bingo arrived in 1979. North Carolina also legalized a uniquely local form — "beach bingo," low-stakes games with prizes capped at $10 — conducted without a license. The Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians opened Harrah's Cherokee on November 13, 1997; a 2012 compact added Class III table games. Harrah's Cherokee Valley River opened in September 2015. The Catawba Nation opened Two Kings Casino Resort in Kings Mountain in July 2021.

Voters lifted the constitutional ban in November 2005 by approving the North Carolina State Lottery Act. The first scratch ticket sold March 30, 2006. Net lottery proceeds fund Pre-K, class-size reduction, school construction, and need-based scholarships. After Murphy v. NCAA, the 2019 legislature authorized in-person tribal sports betting; the first sportsbook opened at Harrah's Cherokee on March 18, 2021. Statewide mobile sports wagering followed via House Bill 347 of 2023. Mobile sports betting launched March 11, 2024, with eight licensed operators. North Carolina has no commercial casinos. House Bill 347 also authorized pari-mutuel horse racing, but that has not yet been activated.
