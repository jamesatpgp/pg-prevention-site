Connecticut's first known lottery was a 1747 grant to Yale College — a public-purpose carve-out from colonial anti-gambling laws that otherwise stood for more than two centuries. The state stayed restrictive into the modern era. Public Act 71-865, signed in 1971, was the pivot: it authorized a state lottery, pari-mutuel wagering, off-track betting, greyhound racing, and jai-alai through later regulation. The Connecticut State Lottery sold its first ticket on February 15, 1972. Two greyhound tracks and three jai-alai frontons operated through the 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s, but all closed by 2001. Live greyhound racing was formally banned by statute in 2017.

The defining moment came through tribal recognition. The Mashantucket Pequot Tribal Nation received federal recognition in 1983. Foxwoods Resort Casino opened on February 15, 1992, with table games only; slot machines were added in 1993 under a state agreement giving the tribe slot exclusivity. The Mohegan Tribe was recognized in 1994 and opened Mohegan Sun on October 12, 1996. Modern expansion came through Public Act 21-23, signed May 25, 2021. The act authorized retail and online sports betting, online casino gaming, and online lottery — all routed through three master licenses held by the two tribes and the state lottery. Retail sports betting launched September 30, 2021, and online gaming followed in October 2021.
