# West Virginia — Gambling History

## Present-day posture

West Virginia in 2026 operates one of the most comprehensive gambling markets per capita in the United States, regulated almost entirely through a single agency: the West Virginia Lottery. The market consists of four racino-model commercial casinos (Hollywood Casino at Charles Town Races, Mountaineer Casino, Wheeling Island, Mardi Gras Casino), the Greenbrier Casino Club private resort casino, **live commercial greyhound racing** at Wheeling Island and Mardi Gras (uniquely — West Virginia is the **only U.S. state where commercial greyhound racing continues to operate**), commercial pari-mutuel horse racing at Charles Town and Mountaineer, retail and statewide-mobile sports wagering (live since August 2018), and a full iGaming online-casino market (live since July 2020). The Limited Video Lottery network places age-restricted VLTs in bars and fraternal organizations under the 2001 LVL Act, capped at five machines per location. The state has no federally recognized tribes and accordingly no IGRA tribal gaming.¹

## Compressed history

West Virginia separated from Virginia and was admitted as the 35th state on June 20, 1863. The new state inherited Virginia's anti-lottery framework and operated under tight prohibitions through most of the 20th century. Pari-mutuel wagering on horse racing was legalized in 1933 — the Depression-era window that also produced legalization in Texas and Washington. Wheeling Downs opened in 1937; Waterford Park (later Mountaineer) and Charles Town Races followed. The 1975 dog-racing bill, passed in response to a request from then-Wheeling Downs president Joe Cresci, allowed Wheeling Downs to convert to greyhound racing in 1976. Tri-State Greyhound Park (now Mardi Gras Casino) opened in Cross Lanes in 1985.² **West Virginia remained the last U.S. state with operating commercial greyhound racing after Florida's December 31, 2020 ban and Arkansas Southland's December 31, 2022 closure.** The industry continues to receive substantial racino-subsidy support via the Racing Modernization Fund / Greyhound Breeding Development Fund mechanisms; multiple legislative efforts to phase out the industry have failed. A U.S. House farm-bill provision passed in April 2026 would impose a federal ban, but had not been enacted as of mid-2026.³

The state lottery was authorized by constitutional amendment in November 1984, enabling legislation followed in 1985, and the first scratch tickets sold January 9, 1986. The Racetrack Video Lottery Act of 1994 authorized VLTs at the racetracks — beginning September 1994 at Mountaineer, Wheeling, and Mardi Gras, and September 1997 at Charles Town. The Limited Video Lottery Act of 2001 extended the VLT model to bars and fraternal organizations. Live table games were authorized at the racinos by local-option referendum 2007-2008, with table games launching July 1, 2008 — making West Virginia the second eastern-U.S. jurisdiction (after Pennsylvania) to authorize racino-based table games. The Greenbrier Casino Club opened in 2010 under separate statutory authority as a private resort casino.⁴ West Virginia was an exceptionally early sports-betting mover: SB 415 passed in March 2018 anticipating the *Murphy* decision; retail sportsbooks opened in August 2018, and online sports betting launched in December 2018 (with the short-lived BetLucky platform, which closed in March 2019 amid contractual disputes). iGaming was authorized under the West Virginia Lottery Interactive Wagering Act of 2019, with operations going live in July 2020.⁵

## Regulatory framework today

The **West Virginia Lottery Commission** (https://wvlottery.com/) is the consolidated regulator for lottery games, racetrack video lottery, limited video lottery, racino table games, sports wagering, and iGaming under W. Va. Code Ch. 29, Art. 22-22D. The **West Virginia Racing Commission** regulates pari-mutuel horse and greyhound racing. The **West Virginia State Tax Department** licenses charitable bingo and raffles under W. Va. Code Ch. 47, Art. 20-21. The **West Virginia Department of Health and Human Resources** funds problem-gambling treatment via the Problem Gambling Treatment Fund; **First Choice Services**, a Charleston-based nonprofit, operates the West Virginia Problem Gamblers Help Network (1-800-GAMBLER / 1-800-426-2537) — one of the longest continuously operating dedicated state problem-gambling helplines in the U.S., dating to 2000.⁶

## Voluntary self-exclusion (VSE)

West Virginia operates **multiple parallel self-exclusion programs**, all administered by the West Virginia Lottery but covering different segments of the market. The architecture is more granular than in any peer state:⁷

1. **Casino / Racetrack / Sports Wagering / iGaming Self-Exclusion** — A unified "Voluntary Self-Exclusion" form (https://wvlottery.com/iplay-with-purpose/self-exclusion) covers all four commercial racinos (Hollywood, Mountaineer, Wheeling Island, Mardi Gras), the Greenbrier Casino Club, all online iGaming platforms, and all mobile and retail sports-wagering apps regulated by the Lottery. Enrollment requires completion of a notarized form mailed to David Bradley, West Virginia Lottery, 900 Pennsylvania Avenue, Charleston, WV 25302. The form covers live greyhound racing and pari-mutuel wagering at the racinos.

2. **iLottery / Lottery Online Self-Exclusion (iPlay)** — A **separate** self-exclusion that applies only to iPlay digital lottery products. An iPlay self-exclusion does **not** cover sports betting, iGaming, or casino activities; those require a separate enrollment under (1). This is the distinguishing structural quirk of West Virginia's system: lottery-online exclusion and casino/sports exclusion are not unified, and an individual seeking comprehensive coverage must enroll in both.⁸

3. **Limited Video Lottery (LVL) bars/fraternal organizations** — The decentralized LVL network has no unified state-administered VSE list; an excluded person's status under (1) does not automatically reach LVL host establishments. Lottery enforcement is conducted at the establishment level, and the Lottery operates retail-level responsible-gaming notice requirements.

4. **Pari-mutuel-only at off-track wagering / racetracks** — Live greyhound and horse-racing wagering at the racinos is covered by (1), but pari-mutuel exclusion at non-racino off-track facilities is handled at the venue level.

For (1), the casino/racetrack/online program, West Virginia uniquely **does not offer a menu of fixed durations**. Once enrolled, the individual is excluded **until they affirmatively request removal**, and removal is permitted only after **at least one full year** from the date of enrollment. There is no automatic expiration; this is closer to the "permanent unless lifted" model used by New Jersey before its 2020 reforms than to the fixed-duration models used by Tennessee, Vermont, or Virginia. The coverage is comprehensive across in-person and online: by enrolling, the individual is excluded from all four racetracks/casinos and the Greenbrier, plus all mobile applications and online gaming associated with the West Virginia Lottery.⁹

Penalties for breach are severe: any attempt to enter a casino or racetrack after enrollment constitutes criminal trespass (no right to appeal), with the individual removed from premises and subject to prosecution. Online breach attempts result in account closure, balance forfeiture, and winnings forfeiture. Licensees that knowingly accept wagers face administrative penalties under W. Va. Code § 29-22A et seq.

West Virginia does not participate in any formal multi-state self-exclusion compact; the West Virginia Problem Gamblers Help Network (1-800-426-2537) coordinates referral to operator-internal exclusion tools and to NVSEP for additional out-of-state coverage. First Choice Services maintains the helpline at https://www.1800gambler.net/, including a dedicated self-exclusion page that supports enrollees through the WV Lottery process.¹⁰
