# Virginia — Gambling History

## Present-day posture

Virginia in 2026 is one of the newest commercial-casino states and one of the fastest-expanding gambling markets in the country. Four operational commercial casinos (Rivers Casino Portsmouth, Hard Rock Bristol, Caesars Virginia in Danville, HeadWaters in Norfolk) operate under Virginia Lottery Board regulation, with a fifth slot (originally Richmond, reassigned to Petersburg by 2024 legislation) pending. The Virginia Lottery also regulates mobile and retail sports wagering (live since January 21, 2021), iLottery (since July 1, 2020), and historical horse racing (HHR) machines through the Virginia Racing Commission's parallel framework. Colonial Downs in New Kent County (acquired by Churchill Downs Inc. in 2022) is Virginia's only commercial pari-mutuel thoroughbred track. Charitable bingo and raffles are administered by VDACS Office of Charitable and Regulatory Programs. Greyhound racing has never been authorized in Virginia.¹

## Compressed history

Virginia is the original cradle of state-sanctioned lotteries in North America: the Virginia Company of London was authorized by King James I in 1612 to operate lotteries to fund the struggling Jamestown colony — the first English-language public lotteries conducted on the continent. Colonial and early-Republic lotteries funded roads, the College of William & Mary (chartered 1693), and various civic improvements; Thomas Jefferson personally promoted an 1826 private lottery to pay down his Monticello debts. The 1902 Virginia Constitution outlawed lotteries after the discredited Louisiana Lottery model.² Virginia maintained one of the South's most restrictive gambling regimes through the first three-quarters of the 20th century under the Byrd Organization's moral-majority politics. Voters approved pari-mutuel wagering in 1988 and the Virginia Racing Commission was created in 1989. Colonial Downs opened in 1997 and ran live racing through 2013 before a dispute between then-owner Jacobs Entertainment and the Virginia HBPA suspended racing for six years. Revolutionary Racing purchased the track in 2018-2019 conditioned on 2018 HHR-enabling legislation; live racing resumed summer 2019 alongside HHR machines branded Rosie's Gaming Emporium.³ Voters approved a state lottery in November 1987 by a 57-43 margin; the Virginia Lottery began ticket sales September 20, 1988, with proceeds constitutionally dedicated to K-12 public education from 1999 onward.

The watershed expansion was 2020. Senate Bill 36 of 2020 authorized commercial casino licenses in five specifically named cities subject to local-option referendums. On **November 3, 2020**, voters in Bristol (72.4%), Danville (68.7%), Norfolk, and Portsmouth (66.6%) approved; Richmond rejected its referendum in 2021 and again in 2023, and the General Assembly reassigned Richmond's authorization to Petersburg by 2024 legislation. Rivers Casino Portsmouth opened January 23, 2023 as Virginia's first permanent commercial casino, with the others following in stages. Sports wagering launched January 21, 2021. iLottery launched July 1, 2020.⁴ Churchill Downs Inc. acquired Colonial Downs and the Rosie's HHR chain in 2022 in a deal valued at roughly $2.5 billion.

## Regulatory framework today

The **Virginia Lottery / Virginia Lottery Board** (https://www.valottery.com/) regulates lottery, sports wagering, casino gaming, and iLottery under Title 58.1, Chapter 40 of the Code of Virginia. The **Virginia Racing Commission** (https://www.vrc.virginia.gov/) regulates pari-mutuel horse racing and HHR machines under Title 59.1, Chapter 29 and 11 VAC 10-47. The **Virginia Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (VDACS) Office of Charitable and Regulatory Programs** (https://www.vdacs.virginia.gov/food-charitable-gaming.shtml) administers charitable bingo and raffles. The **Virginia Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Services (DBHDS)** administers the Problem Gambling Treatment and Support Fund (PGTSF), funded by statutory carve-outs of casino, sports-wagering, and HHR tax revenue. A stand-alone Virginia Gaming Commission consolidating these functions has been the subject of recurring 2025 legislative proposals.⁵

## Voluntary self-exclusion (VSE)

Virginia's self-exclusion program is the **Voluntary Exclusion Program (VEP)**, administered by the **Virginia Lottery**. The VEP is a unified statewide list covering casino floors at all operational commercial casinos, retail sportsbooks at those casinos, all licensed mobile sportsbook operators, all iLottery products, and (under the Virginia Racing Commission's parallel administrative arrangement) Rosie's Gaming Emporium HHR locations.⁶

Duration options are **2 years, 5 years, or lifetime**. Two-year and five-year enrollments are completed by submitting the self-exclusion form online or by mail to the Virginia Lottery. Lifetime enrollment requires an in-person appointment at Lottery headquarters in Richmond or at one of the operational casino locations; the VEP office is reachable at 804-692-7185 to schedule. The Lottery photographs the enrollee, scans the photo ID, and provides written confirmation of the enrollment. The list is electronically distributed to all regulated venues and mobile-app operators within 24 hours of enrollment.⁷

Removal is automatic at the end of the elected period for 2- and 5-year enrollments — no application or interview is required, although the enrollee must re-verify identity to reopen accounts with licensees. Lifetime enrollees may not be removed under any circumstances; this irrevocability is one of the strictest in the country and was deliberately designed by the General Assembly to give enrollees a credible commitment device. Once submitted, a Self-Exclusion Form cannot be withdrawn until the elected period expires.⁸ An excluded person caught wagering at any Virginia regulated venue or on any licensed mobile app forfeits all winnings, is subject to trespass for in-person violations, and is referred to DBHDS for problem-gambling resources; the licensee that accepted the wager is subject to administrative penalty by the Lottery Board under Virginia regulations governing operator responsible-gaming compliance.

Virginia does not participate in any formal multi-state self-exclusion compact. However, the VEP is unusual in unifying casino floor, retail sportsbook, mobile sportsbook, iLottery, and HHR coverage under a single Lottery-administered list — comparable to the unified models in Pennsylvania and Michigan but more comprehensive than the segmented models in most peer states.⁹ The Virginia Problem Gamblers Helpline (1-888-532-3500) is the principal referral resource. DBHDS published an "Evaluation of Virginia Problem Gambling" in October 2025 documenting the scaling-up of services after the 2021 expansion and reporting that approximately 2,000 Virginians had enrolled in the VEP through mid-2025, with two-year enrollments the most common selection.¹⁰

Charitable bingo, charitable raffles, and pari-mutuel wagering at Colonial Downs's live race meet itself (as distinct from HHR) are not covered by the VEP; for those segments the licensee handles individual exclusion at the venue level.
